People Behind ProteoWizard (so far)

Darren Kessner (CAMM)

Darren is responsible for the design of ProteoWizard, and the initial implementation of the libraries and tools.

Matt Chambers (Vanderbilt)

Matt Chambers wrote the SeeMS interactive viewer, and added most of the vendor-specific reader code.

Brendan MacLean (University of Washington)

Brendan works in the MacCoss Lab at the University of Washington, and has been developing Skyline, a Windows client application for building SRM/MRM methods and analyzing the resulting mass spectrometer data. http://proteome.gs.washington.edu/software/skyline

Robert Burke (CAMM)

Rob works on mspicture, the ProteoWizard pseudo-2D gel image creation tool.

Kate Hoff (CAMM)

Kate is working on ms1 feature finding and matching.

Brian Pratt (Insilicos)

Brian is responsible for integration of ProteoWizard into the Trans-Proteomic Pipeline.

Natalie Tasman (ISB)

Natalie handles ongoing development and distribution of the Trans-Proteomic Pipeline, and has contributed vendor reading code to ProteoWizard.

Parag Mallick (CAMM)

Parag is the web designer (and the PI and Director of Clinical Proteomics for CAMM).
